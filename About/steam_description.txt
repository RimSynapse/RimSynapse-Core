[h1]RimSynapse - Core[/h1]
[b]📚 [url=https://github.com/RimSynapse/RimSynapse-Core/wiki]Official Wiki and Documentation[/url][/b]

[b]The foundational LLM Connector for RimWorld.[/b]

RimSynapse Core is a powerful, asynchronous background engine that natively brings narrative intelligence to RimWorld. It acts as the central brain of the RimSynapse ecosystem, intercepting vanilla events and directly altering the narrative tone of your game through its built-in storytelling mechanics, while also serving as the essential bridge required by all companion modules.

[h2]Meet Aura (The AI Storyteller!)[/h2]
Originally the star of the deprecated StoryTeller module, Aura is now baked directly into Core as your spunky, AI-driven game director! She constantly evaluates the overarching narrative "aura" or tone of your colony (Tragic, Tense, Triumphant, etc.) based on recent events. With her own vibrant, LLM-generated personality, she subconsciously guides every interaction and directly influences how standard game events (raids, quests, drop pods) are generated and contextualized. Your colony's successes and failures directly shape her mood, and she isn't afraid to dynamically shift the story based on how she feels about your progress!

[b]Note:[/b] You must select Aura as your storyteller in-game for the LLM to natively intercept and adjust standard game events!

[h2]Features[/h2]
[list]
[*] [b]Universal Backend Support:[/b] Connects seamlessly to [url=https://lmstudio.ai]LM Studio[/url], Ollama, or any OpenAI-compatible local API. While RimSynapse's focus is on local models, with version 0.4.0, it is THE universal LLM connector!
[*] [b]Background Threading:[/b] Never drops a frame. All LLM requests are queued, processed, and sanitized asynchronously on separate threads.
[*] [b]Dynamic Priority Queue:[/b] Intelligently manages hundreds of requests with an advanced Linux-style top-monitor queue system, prioritizing critical events and starving obsolete prompts.
[*] [b]Auto-Discovery:[/b] Automatically detects your active models and manages keep-alive pings to ensure your LLMs don't sleep when you need them.
[*] [b]GPU Monitoring:[/b] Built-in framework to keep track of local VRAM and processing overhead.
[*] [b]Event Tracking Ledger:[/b] Maintains a rolling, serializable backlog of major colony events. Core automatically snapshots the colony's wealth, nutrition, and every colonist's mood/health at the exact tick the event occurred, providing perfect chronological context to the LLM.
[*] [b]Native Event Interception:[/b] Automatically captures major vanilla game events (marriages, legendary crafts, pawn deaths) via Harmony patches. It also natively intercepts standard storytelling events, raids, and quests to generate rich, contextual, multi-stage narratives via the LLM.
[*] [b]Faction Perception Tracking:[/b] Each faction maintains subjective "Perceived Wealth" and "Perceived Strength" values for your colony, updated only through direct contact (visitors, traders, caravans).
[*] [b]Memory Infrastructure:[/b] Provides the per-pawn memory bank, weighted tag system, and decay engine used by companion mods to give colonists persistent, evolving memories.
[*] [b]Colony Snapshots:[/b] When events occur, the framework captures a snapshot of the colony's wealth, nutrition, and pawn status, providing rich context for AI-generated responses.
[*] [b]Opportunistic Task Scheduling:[/b] A background task manager that feeds low-priority LLM requests (backstory generation, memory creation) into idle GPU cycles without impacting gameplay performance.
[/list]

[h2]A Personal Note[/h2]
AI is incredibly cool, and having a framework to utilize powerful, no-cost local models really appealed to me. RimWorld is relatively light on GPU overhead, making it the absolute perfect candidate to max out those graphics cards by running a local LLM in the background. My goal with RimSynapse is to push the boundaries of emergent storytelling using the hardware you already own.
